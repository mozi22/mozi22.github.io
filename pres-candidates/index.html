The rich candidates of 2020 US Elections

One of the biggest highlights of 2020 apart from covid-19 was US elections. With Joe Biden turning out to be victorious it was indeed an interesting battle, the build up to which consisted of some very awkward and dangerous events never seen in the US politics history before.

US elections are considered as the biggest in the world not only because of the political standing of the country on the global map but also due to the amount of money spent by the candidates just to take over the charge. The election itself had cost a total of $14 billion. Democrats spending $6.9 billion, compared to $3.8 billion for Republicans. 

We’re already aware of the wealth of 45th President of United States mostly accumulated through his real estate business. But he is not the only one in the business. Forbes conducted a detailed research on the other Presidential candidates wealth and came up with the numbers. 

We decided to look at the wealth of the candidates from a cryptocurrency perspective to show how much coins you need to own to become as rich as the candidates. With Michael Bloomberg seeming quite out of reach due his immense wealth (1.84M BTC). A simpler target to beat would be Bernie Senders who himself though surprisingly a millionaire but has a wealth only equivalent to 67.6 BTC only (at the time of this writing).
